Tri-Angle

Tri-Angle is 3 triangular/modular lamps that can be placed at different angles. Tri-Angle consists of three tri-angular shaped pieces that lights up when another modular piece is connected with it.

My Role

For this project, I was in charge of the prototyping and designing of the lamp. I diagramed the plans out for the lamp and laser cutted them out in a workshop. I also did 3-D Models to do an exploded view of the Tri-Angle lamp. I also made a poster to showcase our lamp at the SFU showcase and at the Vancouver Maker Faire.

Concept

The theme for the project is unity and collaboration, the collection of forces together making a whole. We wanted to make something modular and interchangeable. We were required to use two materials within this project which was acrylic for the skeleton of the lamp, and leather for the outside structure.

Technology

Tri-Angle works because each modular piece contains a light and battery pack with an incomplete circuit. The circuit is completed when two or more modular pieces is touching together resulting in light! The circuit is made with conductive threads which is exposed on the surface while holding the leather that binds onto the lamp.
